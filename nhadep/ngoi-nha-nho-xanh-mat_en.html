Small Green Home: Modern Design with Affordable Construction Costs


You’re looking at a small house that’s incredibly smart and comfortable. It’s a modern, space-saving design that’s perfect for Vietnam’s hot and humid climate — and best of all, it’s very affordable to build.

From the aerial view, the house sits neatly inside a lush green garden. The roof is fully covered with solar panels that generate its own electricity. Next to it is a large water tank, clearly for collecting rainwater. The house is surrounded by dense greenery, with a white gravel front yard and natural stone stepping paths. It truly feels like the home is being gently embraced by nature.

Looking straight in from the front yard, you see large sliding glass doors opened wide. The inside and outside spaces blend seamlessly together. Sunlight pours in, and a cool breeze flows through. The living room, dining area, and kitchen are all open-plan with no walls separating them. The furniture is made of light-colored wood, simple and clean, making the house feel surprisingly spacious even though it’s small.

The bedroom is equally light and airy. It features a low wooden bed, two small bedside tables, and a large built-in wardrobe. The ceiling is finished with natural wood, paired with wooden floors and white walls, creating a warm yet breezy atmosphere. A big window beside the bed lets in plenty of natural light.

The bathroom is compact but clean and modern. A glass partition separates the shower area, there’s a white toilet, and the floor and walls use bright tiles. Even though it’s small, it feels airy thanks to the ceiling ventilation system.

Overall, this house perfectly represents the minimalist – sustainable – nature-connected style. It’s not fancy or wasteful, yet it’s still beautiful and comfortable. And because the construction cost is low, it’s exactly the kind of home many young people dream of today: just the right size to live in, easy on the wallet to build, helps save on electricity and water bills, and lets you breathe fresh air every single day.

Don’t you think this house looks adorable and very livable?
