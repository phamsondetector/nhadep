The Meaning of Feng Shui in Construction


Feng Shui is an important part of East Asian culture, especially for Vietnamese people when building homes. It is not only a traditional belief but also helps create a harmonious living space, bringing many practical benefits to the family.

What is Feng Shui and Its Origins?



Feng Shui (風水) originated in ancient China, meaning “wind” and “water”. It is a doctrine that studies the influence of wind directions, air currents, water flows, and the surrounding environment on human life, including fortune and misfortune, health, wealth, and destiny. In construction, Feng Shui emphasizes harmony between humans, housing, and nature, based on the principles of balanced Yin and Yang and the Five Elements (Metal, Wood, Water, Fire, Earth) that mutually generate and control each other.
Today, many people view Feng Shui as a scientific approach in architecture: optimizing light, ventilation, and energy flow to create a comfortable living environment, reduce stress, and enhance mental health.

The Meaning of Feng Shui in Construction


Applying Feng Shui when building a home brings many profound meanings:


1 Creating a harmonious and comfortable living space
Feng Shui helps the house be airy, with good circulation of light and wind, avoiding stagnant or blocked energy. A comfortable space contributes to improving both physical and mental health for family members. A house with “harmonious energy” often feels cozy, peaceful, and full of positive energy.

 
2 Attracting luck, wealth, and prosperity
According to traditional beliefs, a house that aligns with Feng Shui will welcome “prosperous energy,” supporting the homeowner’s career, business, and finances. Conversely, committing Feng Shui mistakes (such as the main door directly facing the back door or the kitchen in the wrong position) can lead to financial loss, declining health, or misfortune. Building a house with proper Feng Shui helps minimize risks and enhance long-term stability.


3 Supporting health and family happiness
Feng Shui focuses on Yin-Yang balance, turning the house into a place that recharges positive energy. For example: a quiet bedroom, a clean and well-oriented kitchen, a solemn ancestral altar… all contribute to warmth, reduced conflicts, and stronger family bonds.


4 Connecting humans with nature
In construction, Feng Shui encourages selecting land with “mountains surrounding and water gathering” (sơn quần thủy tụ), or incorporating elements such as greenery, rockeries, and water features to regulate energy. This is not only visually appealing but also environmentally friendly, aligning with modern sustainable architecture trends.


5 Enhancing aesthetic value and functionality
Feng Shui complements architecture by harmoniously integrating the house’s shape, door orientation, and interior layout. The house is not only beautiful but also convenient, easy to use, and maintain over the long term.
Some basic Feng Shui principles in construction
• House orientation and land topography: Choose a direction suitable for the homeowner’s age and destiny (East Four Mansions or West Four Mansions). Prioritize square, elevated, dry, and airy land; avoid concave, steep, or polluted areas.
• Main door: As the entrance for energy, it should be spacious, bright, and unobstructed by large trees, electric poles, or sharp corners.
• Kitchen and bedroom: The kitchen should “sit in a bad position but face a good direction” (tọa hung hướng cát), and avoid facing the bathroom. The bedroom should be quiet, with the bed not placed under beams or facing the door.
• Ancestral altar: Place it in a high, clean position with a good direction to honor ancestors and attract blessings.
• Ventilation and cleanliness: Avoid clutter, ensure free flow of energy.
In addition, it is necessary to consider the homeowner’s age for construction, choose an auspicious groundbreaking date, and consult reputable Feng Shui experts to avoid unnecessary taboos.


Conclusion


Feng Shui in construction is not superstition but the art of living in harmony with the environment. A house that aligns with Feng Shui becomes a “stable foundation,” supporting the homeowner’s comprehensive development in health, wealth, and happiness. When building a home, in addition to modern design and functionality, take the time to learn and apply Feng Shui appropriately. This not only brings long-term benefits but also shows respect for the nation’s traditional culture.
If you are planning to build a house, combine the opinions of architects with Feng Shui experts to create a home that is beautiful, sustainable, and full of luck!
